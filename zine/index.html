No. 1 — The Missing Measure

1

Hairlines are fold guides. The top row is rotated 180° — trust the fold, not your eyes.

How to fold it

One sheet, six moves. You will need scissors (or a decisive thumbnail) for step three.

  1. Fold in half short-edge to short-edge, then each half back to the middle. Unfold: four columns.

  2. Fold in half long-edge to long-edge. Unfold. Eight panels now hold their creases.

  3. Fold in half short-edge again and cut along the center crease from the fold — a slit across the two middle panels only.

  4. Fold long-edge again, grip both ends, and push toward the middle. The slit opens into a diamond.

  5. Keep pushing until the panels form a plus sign, then wrap them flat around the cover.

  6. Crease everything hard. If the pages land out of order, refold the other way — the zine forgives.

Why a broadside

For a while, people drew together in bars on watercolor paper, and the drawings outlived the evenings. A zine is the same wager in fold form: a story small enough to give away and sturdy enough to grow a culture around. When yours fills up, it becomes canon somewhere — even if the somewhere is one table.
